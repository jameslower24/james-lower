I spent two years living in Ecuador as a missionary and it changed my life.

Places I Lived

  1. Atacames, Esmeraldas
    • A beautiful coastal town
    • Street food I still think about; Encebollado, Bolon de Verde, y Encocado de Camaron
    • First real friendships built entirely in Spanish
  2. Ibarra, Imbabura
    • Incredible city in the Andes
    • Sits below a massive Volcano
    • Famous for its colonial architecture and native influences
  3. Lago Agrio, Sucumbíos
    • Amazon basin frontier town, so much humidity, rain, and wildlife
    • Informal markets that taught me more about economics than any textbook
  4. Quito, Pichincha
    • The capital; 9,350 feet above sea level
    • Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with centuries of history in one walk
    • Going back this summer!

Places I Still Want to Visit

I covered a lot of Ecuador but far from all of it. These are the places I'm going back for:

  1. Galápagos Islands: living proof of evolution, and reportedly unlike anywhere else on Earth
  2. Cuenca: colonial architecture and one of the most livable cities in South America
  3. Baños: hot springs, waterfalls, and the swing at the edge of the world
  4. Cotopaxi: one of the world's highest active volcanoes; I want to climb it
  5. Guayaquil: Ecuador's largest city; I passed through but never really stopped

Quick Facts

Capital City Quito = the highest capital city in the world at 9,350 ft above sea level
Language Spanish (official) · Kichwa · Several indigenous languages
Currency U.S. Dollar — Ecuador dollarized in 2000 after an economic crisis
Famous For Galápagos Islands · Amazon rainforest · Cotopaxi volcano · The equator (Ecuador = equator)
